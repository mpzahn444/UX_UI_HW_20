Minnesota Zoo UX/UI Case Study

Author: Matthew Zahn

Collaborators: Jeremy Davis, Daniel Decker, Damian Wojtowicz

Problem Statement

The process of planning a family outing can take a lot of time and preparation. We believe there is an opportunity to make planning a trip to the Minnesota Zoo easier and more rewarding, and thus improve customer satisfaction and encourage repeat visits.

Objectives and Goals

  • Make planning a trip to the zoo easier for zoo goers
  • Make users more informed of upcoming events and zoo promotions

Product Users

Our target audience are people of all ages who plan trips to the Minnesota Zoo with their family and friends

Quantitative Research

We conducted a quantitative survey using Google Forms with 15 participants that had all visited the Minnesota Zoo.

91%

91% of users would visit the zoo website to check information and hours

63%

63% of users wait to purchase their tickets in person

73%

73% of users check the weather before they begin a day at the zoo

73%

73% of users visit the zoo less than once a year

73%

73% of users would visit the zoo website to check for upcoming events

Qualitative Research

Our team conducted 7 interviews with zoo goers and asked them about their experience at the Minnesota Zoo. Here’s some of the things they said...

"We go to get the kids out of the house and burn energy.”

“I’m disappointed with the lack of description of events and types of tickets.”

“It is important to make sure I have everything I need for the day when I visit with my kids.”
“I didn't know the zoo hosted conservation events but I would be definitely be interested in learning more.”

User Insights from Quantitative/Qualitative Data

  • Users need to know the weather before a day at the zoo
  • Users want to know what programming and events the zoo offers
  • The number one reason for visiting the zoo website is to check the hours
  • Parents need a place to let their kids burn energy so they can have a break
  • Parents need easily accessible information about the zoo because they have enough to worry about already
